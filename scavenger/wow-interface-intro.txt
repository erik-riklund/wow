The winds sang of the clash of swords and the defeat of mighty foes, but they conveniently left out the part where the legendary savior of the world had to collect pocket change from a gnoll’s pockets, or pluck intact eyeballs out of a murloc. What if you had a personal goblin assistant that handled the task of sorting through the pocket lint and broken teeth, leaving the legendary artifacts and valuable reagents to you?


[COLOR=Orange][SIZE=3][B]What does it do?[/B][/SIZE][/COLOR]

Scavenger is a lightweight loot system designed to filter the spoils of war. Instead of mindlessly vacuuming everything into your bags, it collects loot based on defined rules. The system comes with sensible defaults to handle money, common items, reagents and vendor trash. It also includes a frequency tracker to enable looting of items based on habits, sorts items in the loot window based on importance, and enables a shortcut (LSHIFT) to claim all items in the loot window.


[COLOR=Orange][SIZE=3][B]How does it work?[/B][/SIZE][/COLOR]

The system hooks directly into the game's looting mechanics, processing each available slot through a rule-based decision chain to determine whether it should be looted or skipped. For the control freaks and engineers among us, you can write custom Lua rules to define how the system behaves. Want to save every drop of Moonberry Juice, or automatically grab every single piece of cheese? Write a rule, register it, and let the addon do the heavy lifting.

[highlight="Lua"]scavenger.register_loot_rule(
  {
    test = function(slot)
      -- A quick glance: should the rule evaluate this slot?
      -- Use `slot.inspect()` if you need to inspect the slot data.

      return slot.type == Enum.LootSlotType.Item
    end,
 
    evaluate = function(slot)
      -- The verdict: Return true to claim it, false to leave it.
      -- Leaving it blank (not returning anything) lets the next rule in the chain decide.
    end
  }
)[/highlight]
